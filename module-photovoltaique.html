PHOTOVOLTAIC SOLAR MODULE

A photovoltaic solar module is an electric direct current generator made up of a set of photovoltaic cells electrically connected to one another, which serves as a basic module for photovoltaic installations and in particular photovoltaic solar power plants.

Description of a photovoltaic panel

A photovoltaic panel consists of a series of photovoltaic cells, formed of a semiconductor material in two layers, one doped positively (P) and the other negatively (N). It is a PN junction. When an electron is torn off, a "hole" is formed instead, behaving like a positive charge.

The electron and the hole escape on either side of this PN junction (the electrons towards N and the holes towards P), creating a potential difference (what we measure in volts). A photovoltaic cell thus produces direct electric current.
